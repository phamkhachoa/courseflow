# ML and AI Data Domain

This domain owns feature, model, AI usage, evaluation and governed training data products across the
enterprise platform.

## Business Capability

ML and AI provides reproducible feature snapshots, model performance facts, AI usage audit datasets,
training handoff manifests and scoring outputs for data science, ML platform, product teams and risk
operations.

## First Data Products

| Data product | Purpose | First source or contribution |
|---|---|---|
| `gold.ml_feature_snapshot` | Versioned feature snapshot for training, scoring and backtesting. | CourseFlow learner activity, course graph and recommendation interactions. |
| `gold.model_performance_daily` | Daily model quality, drift, fairness and business outcome metrics. | CourseFlow recommendation quality and conversion outcomes. |
| `gold.ai_usage_audit_daily` | AI feature usage, prompt metadata, policy outcome and cost metrics. | Future CourseFlow AI tutor, search or content assistant events. |

## First Consumers

- Data science teams for reproducible training and evaluation.
- ML platform teams for feature store and model registry operations.
- Product teams for next-best-action and personalization use cases.
- Risk operations for model monitoring and automated decision oversight.

## CourseFlow LMS Contribution

CourseFlow LMS contributes recommendation tracking, learner activity, course graph, content metadata,
learning outcomes and the first governed Gold snapshot handoff to ML services.

## Future Product Onboarding

- AI assistant products for prompt, response, safety and usage analytics.
- Search systems for ranking features, query logs and relevance labels.
- Customer success platforms for churn, health and next-best-action scoring.
- Risk scoring products for anomaly, abuse and control monitoring models.

## Domain-Specific Rules

- Training datasets must include snapshot id, feature version, row count, content hash and quality
  outcome.
- Model performance facts must identify model version, evaluation window and metric definitions.
- AI usage data must classify prompts, outputs and safety outcomes according to privacy policy.
- Features used for automated decisions require lineage, approval status and monitoring ownership.
